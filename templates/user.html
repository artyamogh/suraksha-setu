Disaster Preparedness

Be prepared for emergencies with these guidelines. Remember, your safety is our priority.

General Preparedness

  • Keep an emergency kit with food, water, and medicines
  • Know evacuation routes in your area
  • Have a family emergency plan
  • Stay updated via official channels
  • Keep important documents in a waterproof container

Flood Safety

  • Do not walk or drive through floodwater
  • Move to higher ground if necessary
  • Turn off electricity if water enters your home
  • Avoid contact with floodwater (may be contaminated)
  • Disconnect electrical appliances

Earthquake Safety

  • "Drop, Cover, and Hold On" during shaking
  • Stay away from buildings and power lines
  • If indoors, stay there until shaking stops
  • If outdoors, move to an open area
  • Be prepared for aftershocks

Cyclone Safety

  • Stay indoors, away from windows
  • Use helmets for head protection
  • Secure loose objects outside your home
  • Stock emergency supplies for 3 days
  • Follow official evacuation orders

Wildfire Safety

  • Wet your surroundings if possible
  • Cover nose with a damp cloth
  • Close all windows and vents
  • Remove flammable materials near home
  • Know multiple evacuation routes

Power Outage

  • Use flashlights instead of candles
  • Keep refrigerator closed to preserve food
  • Disconnect appliances to prevent surge damage
  • Have a battery-powered radio for updates
  • Check on neighbors, especially elderly
